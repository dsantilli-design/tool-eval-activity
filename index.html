AI Tool Evaluation Framework

1. AI Behavior Type

  • Generative – Creates new content
  • Predictive – Forecasts outcomes
  • Assistive – Aids human tasks
  • Adaptive – Adjusts to learner needs

2. Inputs Used

  • Text – Prompts, documents, or written notes used as source material.
  • Image – Photos, diagrams, or scans that the model interprets.
  • Audio – Spoken responses, recordings, or narration inputs.
  • Structured data – Tables, scores, attendance, and other formatted fields.
  • Student data – Personally linked learner information requiring extra safeguards.

3. Output Type

  • Draft – A first pass that a human must revise before use.
  • Decision recommendation – Advice that may influence instructional choices.
  • Analysis – Patterns, trends, or interpretations drawn from data.
  • Public-facing content – Material shown to students, families, or community.

4. Risks

  • Hallucination – Confident-sounding but incorrect claims or citations.
  • Bias – Unequal treatment or skewed assumptions across groups.
  • Privacy – Exposure or misuse of confidential student/staff information.
  • Overconfidence – Users trusting outputs more than evidence supports.
  • Synthetic realism – AI-made media that looks authentic but is fabricated.

5. Oversight Required

  • Light review – Quick human check for clarity and tone.
  • Careful fact-checking – Verify claims against trusted sources.
  • Professional validation – Specialist review before classroom use.
  • Not appropriate – Risk is too high for this context.
